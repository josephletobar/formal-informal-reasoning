# Presubmission Enquiry Draft

**To:** machineintelligence@nature.com  
**Subject:** Presubmission enquiry: reusable internal computation in language models

Dear Editors,

I would like to ask whether the manuscript “Reusable Internal Computation in Language Models: An ABC Test with Attribution Graphs and Causal Interventions” may be suitable for consideration as an Article in *Nature Machine Intelligence*.

The study develops a controlled ABC benchmark in which simple computations are expressed explicitly, as applied problems and implicitly in structured records. On Gemma 2 2B, attribution graphs show strong within-form recurrence, while graph-selected multilayer paths and individual attention source positions influence answer-directed margins. A held-out active-control test does not support necessity of the recurrent sparse features, and cross-form transfer does not establish operation-specific sufficiency. Residual-state screens on Gemma 2 9B and Qwen3 8B provide cross-scale compatibility evidence without being presented as sparse circuit localization.

The manuscript's central contribution is a falsification-oriented mechanistic framework that separates graph recurrence and in-sample pathway influence from evidence for a reusable reasoning module. The public research release, including the benchmark, derived data, figures and verification scripts, is available at https://github.com/josephletobar/formal-informal-reasoning/tree/v0.1.3-abc-publication.

I would be grateful for an editorial view on whether this combination of controlled benchmark design, attribution-graph analysis and explicitly reported negative causal controls is within the journal's scope.
